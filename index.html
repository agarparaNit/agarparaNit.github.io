Incubation center of Narula

The aim of the incubation centre would be to encourage the budding UG/PG students of NIT to take up the challenge of entrepreneurship as career. This would encourage innovation in thought process and professional skills/practices required for market survey, production and commercialization vis-à-vis to assist technology business start-ups in their nursery stage. Thus, the enthusiastic students coming with innovative or new ideas could be given proper platform to prepare appropriate scheme for translating their ideas into marketable product. Implementing an idea requires financial support; but, students in general, lack knowledge on the funding agencies and the processes involved to approach them. Moreover, even if someone succeeds to implement an innovative project with small investment, it would be difficult for him/her to protect the right for the idea or to commercialize the product to start a profitable business with that product. The proposed NIT incubation centre (NIC) is poised to provide the following services:
1) To check and scrutinize the feasibility and commercial viability of the business idea or project plan submitted by any student(s). Based upon the recommendation of expert commettee, student(s) incumbent with their proposal would be registered in the incubation centre through a prescribed agreement containing details of terms and conditions.

2) To provide necessary workspace, infrastructure and facilities as laid down in the terms and conditions.

3) To provide technical support to implement the business idea. Incubation can provide training for business and technical skill development for startup business. Moreover, final year students may get involved in a specific project to provide technical support without any financial clause.

4) To provide support for market survey and commercialization of the product.

5) To provide business networking and alliance.

6) To provide access to professional consultant and expert.

7) To provide access to JIS Group exhibition pavilion to display their innovation/product.

8) To provide IPR support.

9) To provide information regarding financial assistance from industry/ government agencies through their programs to support the budding entrepreneurs. Some of programs are:

a) India Innovation Growth Program b) Millennium Alliance c) TIME IS d) GITA e) NSTEDB f) ISBA (provide technical support only)

The amount of funding grant ranges from Rs,10 lacs to Rs.10 crore depending on the scale of the projects. Several industries like CSIR TECH, Venture capital are there to finance an innovative project on a partnership basis. Institute may provide support in patent filing and patent searching through professionals. A physical space of 2000-3000 sqft carpet area is required for establishing the incubation centre to accommodate office, workshop and a cluster of small chambers (10 sqft) for each entrepreneur for their initial business startup. The incubation centre would consist of
1) Innovation cluster 2) IPR support centre.

An entrepreneur would be initially in two years agreement with NIT to get support from the incubation centre. After 2 years, the agreement may be renewed with new terms and conditions which include payment of incubation centre service charge by the entrepreneur for using its support with maximum limit of another 3 years. The proposed Incubation Centre would be a centralized inter-disciplinery cell in NIT with the following general areas for entrepreneurial services and research:

Engineering applications

IT enabled services

E-Business
